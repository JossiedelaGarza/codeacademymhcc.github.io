# Resources

Here you'll find a list of resources that might help you in pursuing a career in tech. This includes events, organizations and even links to other progams.

# Events in Portland

Tech meetups, talks and other events are good places to learn about specific technologies, meet people at local companies and network with other people in the industry. Here are some good places to consider looking in Portland:

  • Calagator — This is a very comprehensive list of tech-related meetups happening in Portland. It’s also an open-source project.
  • Technology Association of Oregon — A nonprofit focused on connecting tech-minded people in Oregon.
  • New Relic Future Talks — Tech-talk events sponsored by New Relic.
  • Mentorship Saturdays — Work with mentors on your specific problems or projects.
  • Free Geek Meetups — Free Geek is a nonprofit, computer building center inSE Portland. They also host lots of classes and events.
  • NewTechPDX
  • Accelerate Biotech & Digital Health PDX
  • Nike Tech Talks
  • PDX WIT
  • Women Who Code
  • AMA - PDX

# Online Resources

  • Lynda — Online courses for a variety of topics, including web development and design.
  • freeCodeCamp — An online learning system, notdissimilar to Treehouse. A good complimentary resource to explore and one we'll draw from for assignments.
  • Lumen Learning

# Tools

  • Bootcamps.in — This website compares programming bootcamps across the country. A good resource if you’re not geographically bound.

# Other Programs

  • LaunchCode — A service that lists learning resources as well as job resources for people exploring “bootcamp” style education courses.

  • The Recurse Center — The Recurse Center is a self-directed, community-driven educational retreat for programmers in NYC. Their page on diversity contains useful information regarding grant eligibility and what to expect from the admission process.

  • Etsy Hacker Grants - A program dedicated to supporting women in technology.

  • Outreachy — Outreachy helps people from groups underrepresented in free and open source software get involved. They offer (3-month paid) internship opportunities twice a year with a number of free software organizations.
